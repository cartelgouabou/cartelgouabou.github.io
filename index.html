About Me

I’m a Senior Data Scientist and AI Researcher at DMS Logistics, with a passion for designing impactful, real-world machine learning systems. My expertise bridges academic research and production-grade engineering — from deep neural networks for medical imaging to generative AI for document intelligence and time series forecasting.

My research background spans cutting-edge topics in computer vision, class imbalance, and AI-assisted diagnostics. During my PhD at Aix-Marseille University, I developed highly robust and explainable deep learning systems for melanoma detection, contributing to more effective diagnostic workflows in clinical environments.

My thesis was conducted under the guidance of Prof. Abdellatif Moudafi, Prof. Jean-Luc Damoiseaux, and Prof. Djamal Merad, alongside colleagues Rabah Iguernaissi and Jilliana Monnier.

I graduated as valedictorian in 2019 from ENSISA (École Nationale Supérieure d’Ingénieurs Sud Alsace, Mulhouse), after earning my engineering degree in 2017 from ENSPD (École Nationale Supérieure Polytechnique de Douala, Cameroon).

Core Expertise: Deep Learning, Computer Vision, GenAI, Time Series Forecasting, MLOps, Image Processing, Pattern Recognition

Select Projects

DMS Logistics: Smart Container Logistics Optimization

As a Senior Data Scientist at DMS Logistics, I lead the design and deployment of AI-driven solutions to optimize container flow management for key logistics players — including shipowners, terminals, and freight forwarders.

  • Main Objective: Enhance operational efficiency, automate logistics processes, and anticipate container flow through intelligent forecasting and optimization models.
  • Key Contributions:
    • Developed a GenAI-powered insight engine using Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to enable clients to query and analyze CO₂ KPIs via natural language.
    • Built a document intelligence pipeline combining OCR and LLMs to automate data extraction from scanned documents.
    • Designed and deployed time series forecasting modules to predict traffic, capacity needs, and terminal appointments.
    • Engineered scalable APIs and microservices using FastAPI, Docker, Kubernetes, and Airflow.
  • Methods & Technologies:
    • Exploratory data analysis on large, heterogeneous logistics datasets.
    • Development of machine learning models for prediction, classification, and optimization.
    • Data pipeline design and integration across multiple internal and external sources.
    • Cloud deployment and MLOps using Scaleway Cloud, Prometheus, and CI/CD workflows.

DIAMELEX Project: Helping to diagnose melanoma by example

Diamelex is a research project funded by the French National Research Agency (ANR) for an amount of 513,950 euros in collaboration with the start-up Anapix medical, the Marseille Cancer Research Center (CRCM) and the Computer Science and Systems Laboratory (LIS). The project concerns the development of a computer aided diagnosis system for melanoma based on deep learning techniques. Arthur Cartel Foahom Gouabou actively participated in this project as a researcher and its research activities in this project led to the publication of five scientific papers in international and national journals and conferences.
